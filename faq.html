Frequently Asked Questions

What happens during MCS meetings?

Each meeting begins with a speech from either Willie Dustice, the club president, or Todd Bonzalez, our vice-president. The speech is usually about future goals for the club and future events. After this speech, the presentations are held, where presents talk about whatever they have prepared (as long as it is relevant to the club). Afterwards, members are served drinks and talk about pens, pencils and other writing implements amongst themselves.

Do I have to be over 18 to attend MCS meetings?

Yes, you must be over 18 to attend MCS meetings as alcohol is served at each meeting.

Do I have to be a member to attend MCS meetings?

No, you do not have to be a member to attend MCS meetings, however you will miss out on member perks, such as being served alcohol at events, being able to vote in club elections, being able to present and receiving the quarterly packages.

How much is membership?

Membership is 20 dollars a month.

How do I become a member?

Contact us or talk to Mike Truk at a meeting.
